Bio

I am a graduate of Lambda School's web development track with a background in computer science and a lifelong learner mentality. At Lambda School I had the choice for back-end to take Node.js or Java Spring. Since Java was my first programming language when I was taking computer science classes at South Seattle College and Western Washington University I jumped at the opportunity to take the Java track. That rekindled my adoration for the language and sparked my desire to write more meaningful and maintainable code.

I am passionate about the tech industry because I believe it is the most exciting industry of our time and I sincerely enjoy helping to build something bigger than what I can build on my own. I love the thrill of completing a task, a sprint in a larger project, and ‘naturally’ entire projects. I also greatly enjoy the feeling of gaining familiarity with new concepts that often seem overwhelmingly complex when I start to learn them.

Outside of tech I briefly attended a culinary school in New York, worked at Macy’s for several years mostly in the cash office and gift wrap departments as well as on the sales floor. I have worked in several different kitchen roles and had a few customer centric office jobs.
